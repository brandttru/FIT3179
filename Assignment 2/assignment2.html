The Trends in Gender and Nationality at the International Math Olympiad

Brandon Truong 33157073

The Internaional Math Olympiad (IMO) is an annual competition for pre-university students from around the world. The first IMO was held in 1959 in Romania. More than 100 countries participate, with each country sending a team of up to six students, plus one leader, a deputy leader and observers. Due to its emphasis on nationality and gender, there can be underlying trends found in the relationships between nationality, gender and results.

Number of Contestants by Gender

 

  • Both genders show a steady increase in number of contestants
  • Males show greater growth in number of contestants
  • Notably, 1968 had no female contestants competing

Medals awarded at the IMO

  • Despite their numerous appearances, European contestants claim more silver and bronze medals than gold
  • China take home more gold medals than any other country
  • China, USA and Russia have been awarded the most gold medals
  • After 1991, Soviet Union countries entered separately

Countries' scores at the IMO

  • Although scores are determined individually, team scores are compared more often
  • In recent years, problems 1 and 4 tend to be better answered

Government Expenditure on Education as a % of GDP

  • Although they are the winningest country, China spends less than its competitors at around 4% per year
  • Australia spends a consistent amount on education
  • Australia spends more than notable European countries as well as China, yet does not have the medals to rival them

Female Participation at the IMO

  • Australia is consistently able to send at least 1 female representative
  • In 2024, all 6 representatives Oman sent were female
